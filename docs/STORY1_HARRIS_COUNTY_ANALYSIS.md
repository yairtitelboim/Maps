# Harris County Profile Analysis - Testing Hypothesis B

## Executive Summary

**Harris County is HYBRID (both producer and consumer), but it STARTED with both characteristics, not built one then added the other.**

This validates Hypothesis B: Counties specialize. Harris is the exception that proves the rule.

## 6. Hypothesis B Test

### Hypothesis B Statement
**"Counties specialize into producers OR consumers. Coordination = transmission between specialized counties."**

### Harris County Analysis

✅ **Harris is HYBRID: 3.36 GW energy + 3 DCs**

**Question: Did Harris START hybrid or BECOME hybrid?**

**Evidence:**
- Houston = oil/gas capital (1900s) → energy infrastructure
- Houston = major metro (7.5M) → talent/customers
- Data centers arrived (2020s) → leveraged both

**Conclusion: Harris STARTED with both characteristics**
- Inherited energy infrastructure from oil/gas industry
- Grew into metro around energy industry
- DCs followed (path dependency)

**This supports Hypothesis B:**
- Harris is exception (started with both)
- Other counties can't replicate (no 100-year energy legacy)
- Specialization is the norm

## 8. What Makes Harris Different

### Harris vs Dallas (Consumer-Only)
- **Dallas:** Metro (talent, customers) but NO energy infrastructure
- **Harris:** Metro (talent, customers) AND energy infrastructure (gas plants)
- **Why:** Dallas never had oil/gas industry → no energy legacy

### Harris vs Brazoria (Producer-Only)
- **Brazoria:** Massive energy (9.54 GW) but NO metro
- **Harris:** Energy (3.36 GW) AND metro (7.5M people)
- **Why:** Brazoria is industrial/energy hub, not a major metro

### Harris vs Hill (Producer-Only)
- **Hill:** Energy (3.23 GW, 76% solar) but NOT metro
- **Harris:** Energy (3.36 GW, 55% gas) AND metro
- **Why:** Hill has renewable energy but no metro characteristics; Harris has baseload energy AND metro

### Harris vs Bexar (Borderline Hybrid)
- **Bexar:** Energy (2.00 GW) AND metro (2M people) AND DCs (3)
- **Harris:** Energy (3.36 GW) AND metro (7.5M people) AND DCs (3)
- **Similar pattern:** Both started with both characteristics (Bexar = military bases + metro)

## 9. Updated Hypothesis B Framing

**"Texas counties specialize into producers or consumers. Transmission connects them.**

**Exception: Houston (Harris County). It's both.**

**Why? Houston built energy infrastructure for oil/gas (1900-2000). Then became a metro (2000-2020). Then added data centers (2020+).**

**It didn't choose to be hybrid. It inherited both roles from history.**

**Other counties can't replicate this. They have to pick: producer or consumer.**

**Unless you're an oil capital that became a metro, you're specializing."**

## 10. Can Counties BECOME Hybrid?

**Test:**
- **Dallas:** Can it add 3 GW of generation? (No - land too expensive, no energy legacy)
- **Hill:** Can it add 10 DCs? (No - not a metro, no talent/fiber)
- **Harris:** Already hybrid (started with both)
- **Bexar:** Already hybrid (started with both - military + metro)

**Result:** You can't BECOME hybrid. You have to START hybrid.

**This validates Hypothesis B:**
- Most counties specialize (producer or consumer)
- Coordination = transmission between specialized counties
- Hybrids are rare exceptions (started with both characteristics)
