# Hill County Comprehensive Analysis - Energy, ERCOT Planning & Data Centers

**Research Date:** December 2025  
**Method:** Perplexity API - 8 comprehensive queries

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive research examines Hill County, Texas from multiple angles:
- Energy infrastructure and generation capacity
- ERCOT transmission planning and grid infrastructure
- Energy demand drivers and projections
- Data center activity and developments
- Energy-data center coordination
- Recent developments (2024-2025)
- Regional context and adjacent county activity
- Future planning and expansion

**Key Finding:** Hill County sits at the intersection of significant energy infrastructure and emerging data center development, with complex coordination dynamics between adjacent counties.

## Research Categories

### 1. Energy Infrastructure

**Query:** What energy infrastructure, power plants, and generation capacity exists in Hill County, Texas?

**Key Findings:**
- **Total Generation Capacity:** ~518 MW solar + 43 MW hydro = ~561 MW total
- **Solar Projects:**
  - **Ash Creek Solar:** 408 MW (Primergy Solar) - operational July 2025, PPA with Microsoft
  - **Three W Solar:** 110 MWac (Mitsui USA, ex-OCI Solar Power)
  - **Midpoint Solar:** 127 MWdc (97.5 MWac) - Meta EAPA contract
- **Hydroelectric:** Whitney Dam/Lake Whitney - 43 MW (shared with Bosque County, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers)
- **Natural Gas:** None in Hill County (closest is Thad Hill Energy Center in adjacent Bosque County)
- **Wind:** 0 MW in Hill County
- **Battery Storage:** No grid-scale batteries identified
- **Key Operators:** Primergy Solar, Mitsui USA, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers

### 2. ERCOT Planning

**Query:** What ERCOT transmission planning, grid infrastructure, or interconnection projects are planned or underway?

**Key Findings:**
- **Oncor Venus Switch - Sam Switch 345-kV Line Project:**
  - **Scope:** Rebuild 81.5 miles of 345-kV lines in Ellis & Hill Counties
  - **Cost:** $118.9 million (Tier 1 reliability project)
  - **Timeline:** Expected in-service May 2026
  - **Purpose:** Address thermal overloads, upgrade to â‰¥1912 MVA ratings
- **SAMSW Generic Transmission Constraint:**
  - **Restriction:** ERCOT restricts new inverter-based resources (solar, wind, batteries) in Hill County
  - **Reason:** Stability risk under multiple-contingency conditions
  - **Impact:** All new IBR projects must satisfy additional requirements or face constraints
  - **Status:** ERCOT evaluating transmission upgrades to resolve constraint
- **765-kV Projects:** None in Hill County (major projects in West Texas/Permian Basin)

### 3. Energy Demand

**Query:** What is the current and projected energy demand in Hill County?

**Key Findings:**
- **Current Annual Consumption:** ~583,000 MWh/year
- **Local Generation:** 908,792 MWh (100% renewables)
- **Export:** ~39% of generation exported (county is net exporter)
- **Major Consumers:** No data centers, crypto mining, or major industrial users identified
- **Utilities:** HILCO Electric (20,205 customers), TXU Energy (12,523 customers)
- **Demand Growth Drivers:**
  - Statewide ERCOT demand could double by 2030 (driven by data centers)
  - No specific Hill County projections available
  - Industrial electrification and AI/data processing are key statewide factors

### 4. Data Center Activity

**Query:** What data center projects, announcements, or developments are happening in Hill County?

**Key Findings:**
- **Hill County:** Perplexity found 0 announced projects (searched by location text)
- **Our Database:** 2 data centers (coordinates confirm, but location text says "Bosque County")
- **Adjacent County Activity:**
  - **Bosque County:** CyrusOne DFW10 at Thad Hill Energy Center - 400 MW, $1.2B, operational Q4 2026
  - **Hays County:** Sabey Data Centers (184 acres), CloudBurst Data Centers (96 acres) near Hays Energy Power Plant
  - **McLennan County:** InfraKey Capital - 1 GW campus in Lacy Lakeview (6 data centers)
- **Regional Context:** Hill County sits between DFW and Waco-Austin corridor, but not in the most intense build-out zones

### 5. Energy-Data Center Coordination

**Query:** Are there connections between energy infrastructure and data center development?

**Key Findings:**
- **Meta's Solar Offtake:**
  - **Midpoint Solar (Hill County):** 127 MWdc - Meta Environmental Attribute Purchase Agreement (EAPA)
  - Links Hill County solar to Meta's Texas data center portfolio (centers located elsewhere)
- **Microsoft PPA:**
  - **Ash Creek Solar (Hill County):** 408 MW - Long-term PPA with Microsoft
  - Full output contracted to Microsoft data centers
- **Adjacent County Models:**
  - **Bosque County:** Calpine-CyrusOne "Powered Land" model - 400 MW dedicated power, co-located with Thad Hill Energy Center
  - **McLennan County:** 1 GW data center campus with coordinated power infrastructure
- **Hill County Role:** Supplies renewable energy/attributes to data centers elsewhere, but no co-located DCs

### 6. Recent Developments

**Query:** What are the most recent energy or data center developments in 2024-2025?

**Key Findings:**
- **Ash Creek Solar (2024-2025):**
  - **408 MWac** - Primergy Solar
  - **Construction:** Started March 2024, operational July 2025
  - **Financing:** $588M project financing + $350M tax equity = ~$1B total
  - **PPA:** Long-term agreement with Microsoft
  - **Impact:** 350 construction jobs, 6 permanent jobs, $100M tax revenue over 30 years
- **Compadre Solar:** 406 MW planned, expected operations December 2025
- **Three W Solar:** 110 MW - sold to Mitsui, operations starting 2025
- **Unnamed Elawan Energy:** 37 MWdc - Google PPA, expected 2026
- **No data center developments** identified in Hill County during this period

### 7. Regional Context

**Query:** How does Hill County fit into the broader Central Texas energy and data center landscape?

**Key Findings:**
- **Hill County Position:** Between DFW (north) and Waco-Austin (south), on I-35 corridor
- **Adjacent County Activity:**
  - **Bosque County (West):** Thad Hill Energy Center + CyrusOne 400 MW campus - major power-anchored hub
  - **McLennan County (Northeast):** InfraKey 1 GW campus in Lacy Lakeview - largest proposed single-site campus
  - **Johnson County:** DFW-proximate industrial growth
- **Regional Pattern:** Hill County is "boxed in" by large power-anchored AI/data center projects in adjacent counties
- **Strategic Role:** 
  - Potential "spillover" location for support facilities
  - Site for renewable projects/batteries serving same ERCOT zones
  - Transmission upgrades from adjacent projects benefit Hill County
- **Competitive Dynamics:** Land pricing, workforce, and infrastructure patterns shaped by adjacent builds

### 8. Future Planning

**Query:** What are future plans for energy infrastructure expansion or data center development?

**Key Findings:**
- **Hill County:** No publicly documented major projects for 2026+
- **Statewide Transmission:**
  - **Permian Basin 765-kV build-out:** Big Hill-Sand Lake project (summer 2030)
  - **Howard-Solstice 765-kV:** 370 miles, San Antonio to Fort Stockton (fall 2030)
  - These projects shape ERCOT grid topology but don't pass through Hill County
- **Statewide Energy Expansion:**
  - ~130 new natural gas plants planned
  - Small modular reactors (SMRs) - X-energy/Dow, Last Energy (30 reactors in Haskell County)
  - TerraPower exploring SMR co-location with data centers
- **Policy Impact:** Senate Bill 6 requires large users to coordinate infrastructure costs
- **ERCOT Capacity Risk:** System demand may overtake capacity 2026-2027 without major infrastructure
- **Interconnection Queue:** 2,500+ projects waiting, 3-5 year typical wait times
